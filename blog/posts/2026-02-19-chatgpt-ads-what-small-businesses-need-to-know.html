ChatGPT Ads Are Here: What Small Businesses Need to Know

Something big just happened in the world of AI and advertising. OpenAI has started rolling out ads inside ChatGPT.

Major brands like Expedia, Qualcomm, Best Buy, and Enterprise Mobility are already testing the waters. The early buy-in? $200,000 minimum for participation.

For most small businesses, that price tag is eye-watering. But this shift is worth watching—and preparing for.

What's Happening

OpenAI began showing sponsored results in ChatGPT responses on February 9th. Right now, it's a small pilot: ads appear in only about 0.8% of user prompts.

But make no mistake—this is the beginning of a new advertising channel.

Who's Advertising

Early adopters include:

  • Expedia — Travel bookings
  • Qualcomm — Tech hardware
  • Best Buy — Consumer electronics
  • Enterprise Mobility — Fleet services
  • The Knot Worldwide — Wedding planning

Why This Matters for Small Businesses

1. Search Is Changing

More people are asking ChatGPT questions instead of Googling. "What's the best laptop for video editing?" "Plan a weekend trip to San Diego." "Find a wedding photographer near me."

Your customers are using AI for research.

2. Discovery Shifts

Traditional SEO isn't dead, but it's no longer the only game in town. Being the top Google result matters less when people ask an AI for recommendations.

3. New Competition

Large brands get first access to AI advertising. They're building relationships with platforms now. When prices drop and inventory expands, they'll have the advantage.

What Small Businesses Can Do Now

Don't Panic—Prepare

The $200,000 barrier isn't permanent. As inventory expands, entry costs will come down. Use this time to get ready.

Optimize for AI Answers

Make your business discoverable when people ask AI questions:

  • Clear descriptions: Explain what you do simply
  • Local presence: Claim your Google Business Profile
  • Reviews matter: Positive signals help AI recommend you
  • Niche focus: Be specific about your specialties

Watch Your Competitors

If local businesses start testing ChatGPT ads, take note. See what works, what doesn't, and how audiences respond.

Diversify Your Marketing

This isn't about abandoning existing channels. It's about adding one more to your mix:

Channel Strength When to Use
Google Ads Intent-based search People searching for you
Social Media Brand awareness Building community
Email Customer retention Existing relationships
ChatGPT Ads (future) AI-assisted discovery Research-phase customers

The Bigger Picture

ChatGPT ads aren't just another ad format. They're part of a larger shift toward AI-assisted decision-making.

People don't just want information—they want answers. They don't just want options—they want recommendations.

Your business needs to be positioned as the answer and the recommendation.

Bottom Line

You don't need $200,000 to benefit from this shift.

Focus on:

  1. Being discoverable — Clear information everywhere online
  2. Building trust — Reviews, testimonials, social proof
  3. Understanding AI — How people use it, what they ask
  4. Staying flexible — Ready to adapt when the channel opens up

The advertising landscape is evolving. Small businesses that prepare now will be ready to compete when opportunity knocks.

Pro tip: Try asking ChatGPT about your industry. What businesses come up? What information does it share? That's your competition—and your opportunity.
