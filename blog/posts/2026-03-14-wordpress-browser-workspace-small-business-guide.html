WordPress Now Runs in Your Browser: Build Websites for Free

🌐💻

Big news for small businesses exploring web development: WordPress just launched my.WordPress.net—a browser-based workspace that lets you build WordPress websites without signing up for an account or paying for hosting.

This is a game-changer for small business owners, freelancers, and anyone who wants to test website ideas without commitment.

The Big Shift

No accounts, no hosting, no commitment. Build WordPress websites directly in your browser—for free.

What is my.WordPress.net?

my.WordPress.net is a new service from WordPress that creates a private workspace running entirely in your browser. You don't need to:

  • Create an account—Just visit the URL and start building
  • Set up hosting—Everything runs in-browser
  • Enter payment details—It's completely free to explore
  • Download software—Works on any modern browser

This lowers the barrier to entry for web development dramatically. You can now prototype ideas, learn WordPress, or build landing pages without any upfront investment.

Why This Matters for Small Businesses

1. Risk-Free Experimentation

Before committing to a website project—whether building it yourself or hiring someone—you can now explore WordPress for free. Test different themes, try page builders, and see if WordPress fits your workflow without spending a dime.

This is especially valuable for solopreneurs and small business owners who want to understand web development before outsourcing.

2. Faster Learning Curve

Learning WordPress used to mean installing software, configuring servers, and managing databases. That's a lot of friction.

With browser-based WordPress, you can focus on learning the platform—not infrastructure. Click around, build pages, and understand how WordPress works within minutes.

3. Quick Landing Pages

Need a quick landing page for a promotion, event, or product launch? my.WordPress.net lets you build and publish faster than ever.

While it's not designed for permanent websites (more on that below), it's perfect for short-term needs:

  • Event registration pages
  • Product launch teasers
  • A/B testing page designs
  • Client mockups and demos

Pros and Cons

✅ Advantages

  • Zero cost to explore
  • No account setup
  • No hosting configuration
  • Works on any device with a browser
  • Great for learning and prototyping
  • Instant access to WordPress ecosystem

⚠️ Limitations

  • Not for production websites
  • Limited storage and bandwidth
  • Private workspace (no public URL)
  • Can't use custom domains
  • Features limited to core WordPress
  • Work is browser-dependent

When to Use Browser-Based WordPress

Perfect For:

  • Learning WordPress—Explore the platform without commitment
  • Prototyping designs—Test layouts and content structure
  • Client demos—Show website concepts before building
  • Quick landing pages—Short-term promotional needs
  • Evaluation—Decide if WordPress fits your business before investing

Not Ideal For:

  • Production websites—You'll need proper hosting
  • E-commerce stores—Limited features for selling
  • Long-term SEO—No custom domains or advanced optimization
  • High-traffic sites—Limited bandwidth and resources

How This Fits Your Web Development Strategy

Think of my.WordPress.net as your sandbox. It's not a replacement for proper web development, but it's an excellent tool for:

  1. Discovery phase—Before hiring a developer, explore what you want
  2. Skill building—Learn WordPress basics without pressure
  3. Rapid prototyping—Test ideas quickly before committing resources
  4. Team collaboration—Share browser-based workspaces with stakeholders

The best approach: Use my.WordPress.net for exploration and prototyping, then migrate to proper hosting when you're ready to go live.

What This Means for the Web Development Industry

This launch signals a broader trend: lowering barriers to web development. Just as AI is making code generation easier, browser-based tools are making infrastructure setup obsolete.

For small businesses, this means:

  • More informed decisions—You can understand web development before outsourcing
  • Better communication with developers—You'll speak the language
  • Faster time-to-market—Prototype and iterate quickly
  • Reduced risk—Test ideas before investing

Bottom Line

WordPress running in the browser isn't revolutionary technology—it's removing friction. And friction is what keeps small businesses from building websites.

my.WordPress.net is now the fastest, easiest way to explore web development. It's free, it's accessible, and it requires zero commitment.

If you've been thinking about building a website but felt overwhelmed by the technical requirements, this is your starting point. Explore, learn, prototype—then when you're ready, we can help you take it to production.
