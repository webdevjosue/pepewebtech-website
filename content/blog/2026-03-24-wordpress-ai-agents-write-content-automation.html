Your AI Agent Can Now Write and Publish Your WordPress Blog

🤖✍️📝

WordPress.com just changed the game: Your AI agent can now draft, publish, and manage your blog content automatically—no dashboard login required. This isn't just a minor update; it's a fundamental shift in how small businesses can handle content production.

Starting this week, WordPress.com added write capabilities to its Model Context Protocol (MCP) integration, giving AI agents like Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, and others the ability to directly modify your site's content.

What This Means

You can now tell your AI assistant to "write and publish a blog post about our new product"—and it will do it, while respecting your site's design, categories, and approval workflow.

What AI Agents Can Now Do

Previously, AI agents could only read your WordPress data—analytics, content stats, site settings. Now they can write, creating a true content automation workflow:

Draft and Publish Posts

Provide a topic or outline, and your AI agent creates the full post with your site's voice and formatting.

Build and Update Pages

Create landing pages, About pages, and other site content complete with your theme's design patterns.

Manage Comments

Approve, reply to, and moderate comments without opening the WordPress dashboard.

Organize Content

Create, rename, and restructure categories and tags across your entire site.

19 New Capabilities, One Simple Interface

WordPress added 19 new writing operations across six content types: posts, pages, comments, categories, tags, and media. The beauty is that you interact with them through natural conversation—no technical knowledge required.

Here are real examples of what you can now ask your AI agent to do:

"I just finished writing this post. Publish it as a draft, categorize it as 'Travel,' add relevant tags, and write me a meta description under 160 characters."

"I want to start publishing recipes on my blog. Set up a 'Recipes' category with subcategories for Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and Desserts."

"Create an About page with sections for our team, mission, and contact info."

"Approve all the pending comments on my latest post and reply to the one asking about pricing."

"Find all images in my media library that are missing alt text and suggest some based on the filename."

Design-Aware Content Creation

One of the most powerful features: AI agents now understand your site's design system before creating content. They can analyze your theme's colors, fonts, spacing, and block patterns, then generate content that matches automatically.

Why this matters: Your AI-generated content won't look like generic filler. It will inherit your brand's visual identity and adapt when you change themes.

Safety First: Built-In Protections

WordPress understands that giving an AI agent permission to modify your site is a big deal. They've built multiple layers of protection:

Approval Required

Every change requires your explicit confirmation. The agent describes what it plans to do before doing it.

Drafts by Default

New posts start as drafts, giving you time to review before anything goes live.

Reversible Deletions

Deleted content goes to trash, where it's recoverable for 30 days.

Activity Log

Every AI action is tracked in your WordPress activity log for full transparency.

Permission Respecting

Existing user role permissions are enforced. Editors can't change settings; Contributors can't publish.

Granular Controls

Enable only the operations you need. Leave everything else off.

What This Means for Small Businesses

1. Dramatic Time Savings

Writing blog posts, managing comments, and organizing content can consume hours each week. AI agents can handle the repetitive tasks—drafting, categorizing, tagging—so you can focus on strategy and customer engagement.

Real impact: A business spending 5 hours per week on content management could reclaim 3-4 hours by offloading drafting, categorization, and comment moderation to an AI agent.

2. Consistent Content Production

The #1 reason blogs fail is inconsistent posting. AI agents can help you maintain a regular publishing schedule without burnout. Even if you only review and refine AI-drafted content, you're producing more than starting from scratch.

3. Better SEO Organization

AI agents can audit your content structure, add missing alt text, organize categories, and suggest internal links—all factors that improve search engine rankings. They'll also write meta descriptions and optimize headings automatically.

4. Scalable Growth

As your business grows, so does your content needs. AI agents scale with you—handling increased comment volume, more complex category structures, and larger media libraries without adding headcount.

How to Get Started

The setup is straightforward and available on all WordPress.com paid plans:

  • Step 1: Go to wordpress.com/me/mcp to enable MCP on your account
  • Step 2: Toggle on the write capabilities you want to use
  • Step 3: Connect your AI client (Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, or any MCP-enabled tool)
  • Step 4: Start creating through conversation

That's it. No plugins, no code, no complex configuration.

Is This Right for Your Business?

This is perfect for:

  • Small businesses that struggle to maintain a blog consistently
  • Marketing teams overwhelmed by comment moderation
  • E-commerce sites needing product descriptions and landing pages
  • Agencies managing multiple WordPress sites
  • Business owners who want to delegate content tasks without hiring

This might not be ideal if:

  • Your brand voice is highly personal and requires human nuance
  • Your industry requires specialized domain knowledge AI lacks
  • You have strict compliance or legal review processes

The Future of Content Management

This integration signals a broader trend: content platforms are becoming AI-native, not just AI-compatible. WordPress.com is betting that AI agents will become as fundamental to content management as smartphones became to communication.

For small businesses, this is an opportunity to compete with larger companies who have dedicated content teams. AI agents level the playing field—giving you access to sophisticated content automation without the enterprise price tag.
